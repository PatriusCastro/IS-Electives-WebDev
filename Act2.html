Modern Seven Wonders of the World

The Great Wall of China

Continent: Asia

Country: China

Description

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, generally built along an east-to-west line across the historical northern borders of China.

History

Construction of the Great Wall began in the 7th century BC to protect Chinese states and empires against raids and invasions. The most well-known sections were built during the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644 AD).

Petra

Continent: Asia

Country: Jordan

Description

Petra is an archaeological city famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. It is known as the Rose City due to the color of the stone out of which it is carved.

History

Founded possibly as early as 312 BC, Petra became the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom. It flourished during the Roman Empire's annexation of Nabataea in AD 106.

Christ the Redeemer

Continent: South America

Country: Brazil

Description

Christ the Redeemer is a colossal statue of Jesus Christ, situated atop the Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro. It is a symbol of Christianity across the world.

History

Construction of the statue began in 1922 and was completed in 1931. It was created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa.

Machu Picchu

Continent: South America

Country: Peru

Description

Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru. It is renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar.

History

Machu Picchu was built in the 15th century but abandoned a century later during the Spanish Conquest. It was rediscovered in 1911 by American historian Hiram Bingham.

Chichen Itza

Continent: North America

Country: Mexico

Description

Chichen Itza is a large pre-Columbian archaeological site built by the Maya civilization. It is known for its step-pyramid, temples, and observatory.

History

Chichen Itza was a major focal point in the northern Maya lowlands from the Late Classic (c. AD 600–900) through the Terminal Classic (c. AD 800–900) and into the early portion of the Postclassic period (c. AD 900–1200).

The Roman Colosseum

Continent: Europe

Country: Italy

Description

The Roman Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater built during the Roman Empire. It is the largest amphitheater ever built and is considered one of the greatest works of Roman architecture.

History

Construction of the Colosseum began in AD 72 under the emperor Vespasian and was completed in AD 80 under his successor and heir, Titus. It was used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles such as animal hunts, mock sea battles, and executions.

Taj Mahal

Continent: Asia

Country: India

Description

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

History

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. It is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture, a style that combines elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles.
